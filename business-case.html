Why it works, not just what it returns

The dollar figures above are the lagging indicators. These are the mechanisms driving them.

🛡️

Trust & Transparency

Showing the formula and the client's own numbers counters the exact complaint that motivated this project — clients distrusting external calculators.

⚡

Reduced Friction

"Can I afford this position?" and "how much can I withdraw?" go from a spreadsheet-and-guess exercise to one screen, live, in the portal.

🏆

Brand Positioning

Competitors ship static, account-disconnected calculators. A live, risk-aware assistant reframes Equiti from utility provider to trading advisor.

🤝

Sales Enablement

Relationship managers can screen-share the All-in-One calculator on a call and let the margin-call zone make the risk-management case visually.

Engagement signals to watch first

These are leading indicators — they show up in analytics within weeks, well before revenue data matures enough to confirm the conversion-uplift assumption.

Expected engagement lift vs. a static calculator page

Top 3 quantified benefits

  • Conversion uplift is the dominant value driver — ~$720K/yr even in the low scenario.
  • Support deflection is real but secondary — $2K–$6K/yr direct savings on a small ticket category.
  • Engagement metrics are the early-warning signal that the conversion assumption is tracking correctly.

Top 3 strategic benefits

  • Trust through transparency directly answers Equiti's own stated client complaint.
  • Repositions Equiti from "broker with a calculator" to trading assistant.
  • Gives relationship managers a reusable, credible visual sales asset.
Bottom line: the build cost of this tool is small relative to even the low-end conversion scenario. The biggest open risk isn't cost, it's that the conversion-uplift assumption is unverified against Equiti's real traffic — the recommended next step is instrumenting the live calculator with basic analytics (tab views, time spent, "save simulation" usage) so this model can be replaced with measured numbers within the first month.
All dollar figures are modeled estimates based on stated assumptions (200K monthly traffic, $800 avg. annual client revenue, 12-agent support desk at $20/hr), combined with general industry benchmarks for interactive-tool conversion uplift (10–30%) and support deflection (20–40%). None of this is yet measured on Equiti's live traffic — treat as a planning model, not a guarantee.
